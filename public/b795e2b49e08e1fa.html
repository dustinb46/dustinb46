Policy

Senate farm-bill draft would lock in regular FMMO “make allowance” surveys

The Senate Agriculture Committee released its draft farm bill (the Agricultural Act of 2026) on June 23, including permanent authorization of mandatory cost-and-yield surveys so Federal Milk Marketing Order make allowances track current manufacturing costs, alongside Dairy Margin Coverage updates.
Why it matters: Make allowances feed straight into Class III/IV component prices — institutionalizing cost surveys could steadily shift how much of the milk-check value stays with processors versus producers.

Markets · Outlook

Rabobank: the milk-supply surge is set to flip to contraction

Rabobank's mid-June Q2 Global Dairy Quarterly projects Big-7 exporter output ending 2026 just 0.2% above prior year (down from +2.6% in 2025), with global production essentially flat in Q3, down 1.6% in Q4, and a roughly 0.2% full-year decline in 2027 — the first annual contraction since 2022.
Why it matters: The oversupply that has capped farmgate prices is forecast to rebalance, a potential turning point for milk prices and margins heading into 2027.

Animal Health · Regulation

Interstate cattle moves now hinge on your state's H5N1 status

Under USDA's National Milk Testing Strategy, lactating dairy cattle leaving states that have earned “Unaffected State” status no longer require pre-movement H5N1 testing — but states still classified “Affected,” such as California, keep the pre-movement testing requirement.
Why it matters: Your testing paperwork, timing, and cost for moving cows across state lines now depend on where you and your buyers sit on the status map.

→ aphis.usda.gov — HPAI in Livestock / Secure Our Herds

Animal Health · Watch

Virulent bluetongue (BTV-3) keeps marching across northern Europe

The midge-borne BTV-3 strain is driving outbreaks across Germany, Belgium, France, Luxembourg, and Denmark, with England confirming further cattle cases in June following abortions and milk drop; one virologist warns it is spreading fast “despite vaccination.”
Why it matters: BTV-3 cuts milk yield and fertility and is reshaping European herd economics and trade — a vector-borne incursion risk worth tracking even from North America.

→ gov.uk — Bluetongue: latest situation

Policy · Legal

Wisconsin farmers sue USDA to halt checkoff funding of sustainability programs

The Wisconsin Institute for Law & Liberty filed suit this month on behalf of three dairy farmers, arguing the mandatory dairy checkoff unconstitutionally bankrolls the Innovation Center for U.S. Dairy and its Net Zero Initiative and FARM Environmental Stewardship programs.
Why it matters: If it advances, the case could disrupt the checkoff every producer pays into and the industry's net-zero infrastructure built on those dollars.

Competition · Biotech

Capital flows back into precision-fermentation milk proteins

Standing Ovation raised $34.2M to commercialize precision-fermented casein in North America during 2026, while Perfect Day's whey-protein facility in Gujarat, India targets a second-half 2026 startup — signs investor appetite for animal-free dairy proteins is recovering.
Why it matters: Scaling animal-free casein and whey is a slow-building competitive and pricing threat to commodity milk proteins and the U.S. whey export market.

→ agfundernews.com — Standing Ovation nets $34M for U.S. casein launch

Technology · Calf Health

An NSF-funded AI system aims to catch calf pneumonia before you can

Penn State's “CalfHealth” project pairs low-cost wearable accelerometers and precision robotic feeders with a non-contact, Wi-Fi-based breathing sensor to flag respiratory illness in calves early using combined behavior and breathing data.
Why it matters: Respiratory disease is a leading driver of calf treatment cost and mortality; cheap, automated early detection could trim losses and antibiotic use.

→ psu.edu — AI-enabled monitoring for dairy calf health

Markets · Risk

Class IV and butter pop even as milk stays heavy

Class IV futures rebounded sharply on June 26 — some contracts settling limit-up (+$0.75) — with spot butter moving higher, a notable divergence given U.S. milk output remains abundant.
Why it matters: Butterfat/Class IV strength running against a heavy-supply backdrop is a hedging and revenue-mix signal worth weighing on forward contracts.
