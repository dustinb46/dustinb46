01 · Herd & Genetics

Replacements

Heifer relief won't arrive until 2027 — and the beef check, not the milk check, is driving breeding math

CoBank's new outlook projects roughly 438,800 fewer replacement heifers entering U.S. dairy herds this year — the tightest supply since 1978 — with a slow rebuild beginning only in 2027–28; lead economist Corey Geiger notes net margins on many farms now run on the beef check, not the milk check. Why it matters: with replacements above $3,000/head, the breed-for-beef-vs.-dairy decision, extended cow longevity and heifer-raising contracts stay locked in for at least two more years.

02 · Animal Health & Policy

H5N1

A bad H5N1 year could cost dairies more than federal aid will reimburse

New economic modeling reported by Feed Strategy finds a worst-case HPAI outbreak in dairy cattle could erase close to 1% of U.S. GDP, and warns that thin indemnity payments may actually discourage farms from investing in the biosecurity that slows spread. Why it matters: if your milk-loss and culling exposure outruns the federal payout, the cost-benefit case tilts toward funding biosecurity yourself rather than counting on reimbursement.

03 · Processing & Competition

Consolidation

Lactalis folds South Island logistics into one mega distribution hub

Fresh off its NZ$4.2B purchase of Fonterra's Mainland consumer business, Lactalis New Zealand has opened "The Yard," a single multi-temperature hub in Christchurch that collapses fresh, chilled, frozen and ambient dairy flows into one footprint. Why it matters: a leaner, more vertically integrated world-#1 processor sharpens export competition on the cheese, butter and powder lines U.S. exporters are chasing in the same Asian and Latin American markets.

04 · Funding & Value-Add

Grants

$2.9M in value-added grants land with 28 small Midwest dairy businesses

The USDA-funded Dairy Business Innovation Alliance awarded its latest Dairy Business Builder grants (up to ~$100K each) to 28 farmstead creameries, cheesemakers and processors across 11 states — 64% of them first-time recipients. Why it matters: it's a live, recurring capital channel for on-farm diversification, value-added products, by-product use and export expansion; the next application window is worth flagging now.

05 · Global Structure

Attrition

Britain's dairy farm count slips below 7,000 for the first time

AHDB figures show Great Britain lost about 160 dairy producers in six months, falling to 6,850 — a 15% decline over five years — as weak winter farmgate prices and rising environmental compliance push exits. Why it matters: structural attrition among Western producers caps global milk-supply growth and previews the cost and regulatory pressures already drifting toward U.S. operations.

06 · Trade

Exports

U.S. dairy exports set another record in April, led by cheese

April export volume rose 15% year-over-year in milk-solids-equivalent terms — the seventh straight monthly gain — with cheese setting a fresh monthly record on surging shipments to Mexico (+28%) and South Korea (+69%). Why it matters: export pull is now the primary outlet absorbing record U.S. milk output, a key prop under Class III/IV even as domestic spot cheese softens.

07 · Inventories

Cold storage

Butter stocks tighten while cheese sits flat in the latest cold-storage read

USDA's newest monthly Cold Storage report shows butter holdings down roughly 6% year-over-year (about 354 million lbs) while total natural cheese inventory is essentially flat near 1.41 billion lbs. Why it matters: leaner butter stocks underpin butterfat values, while an ample cheese stockpile caps block/barrel upside — a split signal for component-priced milk checks.
