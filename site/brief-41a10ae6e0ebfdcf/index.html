01Labor

USCIS cracks the H-2A door open for year-round dairy labor

A June 17 USCIS policy memo clarifies that dairy roles can qualify for H-2A even though the work is year-round — eligibility now turns on whether the employer's need is temporary or seasonal, not on the job itself. If it holds, it's the first real federal crack in dairy's exclusion from the only uncapped ag-visa program, and a potential pressure valve for a labor market squeezed by enforcement.

Source → USCIS — H-2A Temporary Agricultural Workers

02Revenue · Manure

Manure biogas refined into jet fuel in a claimed world-first pilot

Circularity Fuels says it ran raw biogas from a Madera, California dairy digester (a 5,000-head site) all the way to ASTM-spec sustainable aviation fuel in a six-month on-farm pilot — at roughly one-fifth the capital cost of European SAF plants now under construction. The angle for operators: a credible new off-take for digester gas beyond stalled carbon credits and LCFS, with a commercial site targeted for 2027.

Source → Brownfield Ag News — Turning dairy manure into jet fuel

03Regulatory · Checkoff

Lawsuit challenges where your checkoff dollars go

Dairy farmers, backed by the Wisconsin Institute for Law & Liberty, are suing USDA and the National Dairy Promotion and Research Board, arguing the mandatory checkoff (15¢/cwt, ~$300M/yr) unconstitutionally funds sustainability/ESG work via the Innovation Center for U.S. Dairy. Whatever the merits, a First Amendment win would reshape how every producer's promotion dollars can be spent.

Source → Brownfield Ag News — Lawsuit challenges use of dairy checkoff funds

04Genetics · Genomics

First Milk wires satellite nutrient mapping into 700 supplier farms

The UK co-op is layering satellite-led risk mapping (Senus) over per-farm nitrogen and phosphate footprinting (Farm Carbon Toolkit) across its ~700 members to target where nutrient loss meets waterway risk. It's a preview of where processor-driven, field-level sustainability data requirements are heading — the kind of supplier mandate that tends to cross the Atlantic.

Source → Co-operative News — First Milk launches water quality project across 700 farms

05Pricing

Class I base price slips for the first time since February

USDA's advanced Class I base price for July, announced June 17, fell 85¢ to $21.33/cwt — still $2.51 above July 2025, but the first month-over-month decline this year. A useful tell that the front-month strength that's propped up fluid premiums is starting to roll over heading into the back half.

Source → USDA AMS — Announcement of Advanced Prices and Pricing Factors (PDF)

06Markets · Outlook

Rabobank: the global milk-supply surge is rolling over

Rabobank's Q2 Global Dairy Quarterly calls the top of the cycle: output growth that peaked at 5.2% in Q4 2025 is seen at +1.5% in Q2, flattening in Q3 and turning negative (−1.6%) by Q4, with 2027 set for the first annual decline since 2022. The read for planners: tighter supply and a "sensitive margin balance" where input costs, not volume, drive the next price move.

Source → Rabobank — Global Dairy Quarterly, Q2 2026

07Supply Chain · Spot Milk

New processing capacity is quietly redrawing the spot-milk map

USDA's latest Dairy Market News shows new manufacturing capacity — including ultrafiltration lines in the East — pulling milk away from other classes, even as Upper Midwest cheesemakers with downtime still dump Class III spot loads at steep discounts. The takeaway for anyone selling spot or negotiating a plant home: regional premiums are diverging fast, and your leverage depends heavily on which plants are coming online near you.

Source → USDA AMS — National Dairy Market News at a Glance (PDF)

08Business Continuity

The succession cliff: a quarter retiring, most with no plan

A new state-of-the-industry read finds more than 25% of dairy operators expect to retire within five years while ~60% have no defined succession plan — a setup that turns transfers into dispersals and quietly removes both herds and farmland from production. If your own transition isn't documented, this is the cheapest risk on the list to fix.

Source → Dairy Herd — 60% of Dairy's Future Hangs in the Balance of Succession

09What to Watch

USDA's May Milk Production report lands Monday

NASS releases May Milk Production at 2:00 p.m. CT on Monday, June 22. With cow slaughter running ~4% above last year and the supply debate wide open (see item 06), the milk-per-cow and herd-size lines will set the tone for Class III/IV futures into July. Worth a calendar hold before pricing any near-term sales.
