01 Technology · Manure economics

A dairy digester just made certifiable jet fuel — for the first time, start to finish

Circularity Fuels says a six-month pilot at a Madera, California dairy converted raw manure biogas (~65% methane) straight into ASTM D7566-spec sustainable aviation fuel, hitting >98% methane and >90% CO₂ conversion in a single electrified step, with a commercial site targeted for 2027.

Why it matters: If the claimed capital cost (about one-fifth of European SAF plants) holds, manure stops being purely a disposal cost and digester gas gains a higher-value offtake than pipeline RNG — reshaping the economics of on-farm anaerobic digestion.

02 Global markets

GDT falls 2.8% as Chinese buying sinks toward 15-year lows

Global Dairy Trade Event 406 dropped 2.8% to an average of roughly US$3,979/MT, with China largely on the sidelines and the Middle East and Southeast Asia carrying demand; at the CME, nonfat dry milk has slid sharply — down close to 50¢ in two weeks off its early-May record.

Why it matters: Weak Chinese powder demand plus a falling CME NDM/skim complex pressures Class IV and the mailbox price, and signals that the U.S. surplus is increasingly dependent on an export window that is narrowing.

03 USDA data

Cheese output keeps climbing, but American-style and butter are quietly turning

USDA's latest Dairy Products report shows total cheese production up about 1.7% year-over-year in April, yet American-type cheese slipped for a second straight month and butter churns are showing the first hints of year-over-year contraction after a downward revision to the prior month.

Why it matters: The headline "more cheese" masks a demand split — softening American/Cheddar output alongside record-leaning butterfat tells you where make capacity and component value are really flowing into Class III pricing.

04 Competitive · Global value chain

Foreign capital is buying into New Zealand's milk — at the processing end

Overseas investors are bypassing farmland to fund high-tech protein-extraction assets in New Zealand (Singapore-based OFI is adding a second dryer at Tokoroa), capturing the downstream margin on milk protein concentrates for clinical and sports nutrition as Fonterra divests consumer brands.

Why it matters: The world's largest dairy exporter is being re-tooled toward high-value protein ingredients — the same premium categories U.S. processors are chasing, raising the competitive bar for component and ingredient revenue worldwide.

05 Processing footprint

Agropur to shutter a Maritime plant and pour $20M into another

Agropur will wind down its Sussex, New Brunswick plant by 2028 (about 60 jobs) and invest roughly C$20 million to expand Miramichi by 50% into the region's main fluid hub, with butter and protein-ingredient work shifting to a modernized Nova Scotia site.

Why it matters: A textbook consolidation move — fewer, bigger, more specialized plants — that redraws milk-routing and hauling for nearby producers and signals where co-op processors see scale economics heading.

Source: CBC News →

06 Regulatory · Manure handling

An unattended manure pump cost one farm $270,000

An Ontario dairy (Henro Dairy Farms) was fined $270,000 plus a $67,500 surcharge under the Ontario Water Resources Act after an unattended manure pump overflowed and caused a fish kill in a nearby creek.

Why it matters: A concrete, six-figure precedent on land-application and lagoon-transfer supervision — cheap insurance to review pump monitoring, alarms, and field-spreading SOPs before an inspector sets the price.

Source: eDairyNews →

07 Co-op finance

An Irish co-op is paying suppliers a weather bonus — worth watching as a model

Tirlán is distributing a €4.5 million support payment to milk suppliers (an extra 0.75 cents/litre on May volumes), part of a broader €5.5 million package with feed, fertilizer, and calf-milk-replacer subsidies after a brutal wet spring.

Why it matters: A live example of a cooperative using retained earnings as a producer-retention and risk-sharing tool — a benchmark for what "co-op support" can look like when margins compress.

Source: Agriland →

08 Food safety · Product quality

A high-protein milk recall flags an over-fortification risk

Lactalis Canada recalled Lactantia UltraPur 2% "20g Protein, Lactose-Free" milk sold at Costco nationwide after observed over-fortification of vitamins A and D above recommended intake limits.

Why it matters: As the value-added, high-protein and fortified milk category booms, the failure mode shifts from spoilage to dosing accuracy — a reminder that fortification QC is now a real product-liability surface for branded dairy.
