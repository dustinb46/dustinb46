Labor & Policy

An H-2A overhaul that would finally cover dairy

House Agriculture Committee Chairman Glenn "G.T." Thompson says he will introduce the Securing Agriculture's Workforce Act this month. It would let H-2A guest workers fill year-round jobs — treating any contract under 350 days as "temporary" — and cut the cost and paperwork of hiring foreign workers.

Why it matters: Dairy has been largely locked out of H-2A because the program is seasonal-only. Year-round eligibility plus wage reform could reshape labor cost and availability for milking operations.

Logistics

Pennsylvania panel clears heavier milk trucks

The Pennsylvania House Transportation Committee unanimously advanced a bill raising milk hauler weight limits to 95,000 lbs on five axles, 107,000 lbs on six axles (with a permit), and 117,000 lbs on seven axles (with a permit) — up from today's 80,000 lb cap.

Why it matters: Heavier loads mean fewer trips and lower per-hundredweight hauling cost. If enacted it narrows a competitive disadvantage Pennsylvania shippers face against neighboring states — a template other dairy states are watching.

Markets

The milk-powder rally looks fragile

Nonfat dry milk spot prices have surged in 2026 on tight stocks, but Daily Dairy Report analyst Sarina Sharp warns that record-pace cow additions and new processing capacity will lift skim and NDM output later this year — and U.S. powder exports are already softening as buyers find cheaper origins.

Why it matters: Operators leaning on elevated Class IV and powder values should plan for a possible second-half reversal in the milk-powder complex.

Processing

A co-op locks in branded cottage cheese demand

The Michigan Milk Producers Association acquired a plant in Remus, Michigan dedicated to producing Good Culture's cottage cheese line, after the brand nearly quadrupled sales and helped drive roughly 60% category growth over three years.

Why it matters: A farmer-owned cooperative is securing high-growth, branded demand for members' milk — a concrete signal of where fluid volume is finding a premium home as the cottage cheese boom continues.

Competition

Danone sues Chobani over protein-label math

Danone (Dannon/Oikos) sued Chobani in Manhattan federal court, alleging its "20G Protein" tubs reach 20 grams only by using a 6.7-ounce serving rather than the standard 5.3 ounces — which would otherwise show about 18 grams.

Why it matters: The fight over serving-size math in the booming high-protein dairy category could force label changes and shows how aggressively protein claims are now being policed.

Manure & Energy

First on-farm run of dairy biogas to jet fuel

Circularity Fuels says it completed a six-month pilot converting raw manure-digester biogas into ASTM-spec sustainable aviation fuel on-site at a 5,000-head dairy near Madera, California, using skid-mounted reactors at roughly one-fifth the capital cost of conventional plants (modeled carbon intensity of −350.7 gCO₂e/MJ).

Why it matters: On-site fuel from manure could turn a methane liability into a revenue stream without pipeline access — a new manure-economics option for larger herds to track.

Animal Health

USDA puts $105M into 40 screwworm projects

APHIS awarded about $105 million to 40 "Grand Challenge" projects — selected from 226 proposals — to expand sterile-fly production, detection, and treatment, as confirmed U.S. New World screwworm cases reached 12.

Why it matters: Sterile-fly capacity and faster detection are the front line protecting cattle herds; the scale of funding signals USDA expects a prolonged fight near the southern border.

Trade · Background

EU Parliament ratifies the EU-US trade deal

The European Parliament voted 440–151 (50 abstentions) to implement the EU-US agreement, removing EU tariffs on most U.S. industrial and many agricultural goods while the U.S. caps its tariffs on EU imports at 15%. Formal endorsement by all 27 member states is still pending.

Why it matters: Lower EU duties and preferential access could open room for U.S. dairy ingredient and product exports — watch the dairy-specific tariff lines as implementation proceeds.
