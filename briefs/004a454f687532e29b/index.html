Policy

The Senate's farm-bill draft would make FMMO "make allowance" surveys permanent

The Senate Agriculture Committee released its draft farm bill (the Agricultural Act of 2026) on June 23. Among the dairy provisions: permanent authorization of mandatory cost-and-yield surveys so Federal Milk Marketing Order make allowances stay tied to current manufacturing costs, plus updates to Dairy Margin Coverage.

Why it matters: Make allowances are baked directly into Class III and IV component prices. Institutionalizing regular cost surveys is the mechanism that would let processors' deductions drift upward over time — a slow, structural pull on the share of the milk-check value that reaches the farm gate.

Markets

Class IV and butter pop — even with milk still running heavy

Class IV futures rebounded sharply on June 26, with some contracts settling limit-up (+$0.75) as spot butter moved higher — a notable divergence at a time when U.S. milk output remains abundant and the broader complex has been soft.

Why it matters: Butterfat and Class IV strength running against a heavy-supply backdrop is a hedging and revenue-mix signal. If your blend leans Class IV, the move opens a window to lock forward margin; if you carry Class III hedges, watch the spread rather than assume the whole board moves together.

Animal Health · Watch

Virulent bluetongue (BTV-3) keeps marching across northern Europe

The midge-borne BTV-3 strain is driving fresh outbreaks across Germany, Belgium, France, Luxembourg and Denmark, with England confirming further cattle cases this month following abortions and milk drop. Researchers warn it is spreading fast "despite vaccination," and losses are running into the hundreds of millions of euros.

Why it matters: BTV-3 cuts milk yield and fertility and is reshaping European herd economics, replacements and trade flows. It's a vector-borne incursion and import-risk story worth tracking even from North America, and a reminder to keep midge and biosecurity protocols current.

Competitive · Biotech

Capital is flowing back into precision-fermentation milk proteins

Standing Ovation raised $34.2M to commercialize precision-fermented casein in North America during 2026, while Perfect Day's whey-protein facility in Gujarat, India is on track to start up in the second half of 2026 — signs investor appetite for animal-free dairy proteins is recovering after a lull.

Why it matters: Casein and whey made without a cow remain a slow-building competitive and pricing threat to commodity milk proteins — and specifically to the U.S. whey-and-protein export business that has carried a lot of recent dairy revenue.

Technology · Calf Health

An NSF-funded AI system aims to hear calf pneumonia before you can

Penn State's "CalfHealth" project, backed by the U.S. National Science Foundation, pairs low-cost wearable accelerometers and precision robotic feeders with a non-contact, Wi-Fi-based breathing sensor, then uses AI to flag respiratory illness in pre-weaned calves early from combined behavior and breathing data.

Why it matters: Respiratory disease is a leading driver of calf treatment cost, mortality and lost lifetime productivity. Cheap, automated early detection is exactly the kind of tool that can cut losses and antibiotic use in the calf barn — watch whether it reaches a commercial product.

Regulatory

Interstate cattle moves now hinge on your state's H5N1 status

Under USDA's National Milk Testing Strategy, lactating dairy cattle leaving states that have earned "Unaffected State" status no longer require pre-movement H5N1 testing — but states still classified "Affected," such as California, keep the pre-movement testing requirement in force.

Why it matters: Your testing paperwork, timing and cost for moving cows across state lines now depend on where you and your buyers sit on the status map. Confirm a destination's and origin's current status before scheduling hauls, replacements or cull movements.
