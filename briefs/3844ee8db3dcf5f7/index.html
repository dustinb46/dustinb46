Labor & Policy

Washington cracks open an H-2A door for year-round dairy labor

A new federal guidance memo tells visa adjudicators that dairy jobs can qualify for the H-2A agricultural program despite running year-round — a long-sought clarification, though the statute's "temporary or seasonal" language still caps how far it reaches. For operators squeezed by parlor-staffing shortages and enforcement risk, it is the first credible legal path to H-2A milkers in years — worth pricing into your 2027 labor plan.

M&A · Protein

Danone buys Australia's Made Group for ~A$2B and takes full control of its Saputo JV

Danone is paying about A$2 billion for protein-drink and dairy maker Made Group to chase GLP-1-driven high-protein demand, and is simultaneously buying out Saputo's 49% of their Australian fresh-dairy joint venture. It signals where processor capital is now flowing — functional protein — and reshuffles a major competitor's footprint in a category that is pulling milk solids away from commodity uses.

Processor Footprint

Agropur to shut its Sussex, N.B. plant by 2028, pours $20M+ into Miramichi and Bedford

The co-op is consolidating Atlantic-Canada processing: fluid milk shifts to a modernized Miramichi plant (capacity up ~50%), while Sussex's butter and protein-ingredient lines move to Bedford, Nova Scotia, costing roughly 60 jobs. A concrete reminder of how quickly cooperatives rationalize plants — and of the milk-routing and haul-cost swings that follow a single plant decision.

CTV News — Agropur expanding Bedford, scaling down Sussex
Animal Health · Biosecurity

New World screwworm reaches 15 U.S. cases; states tighten cattle-movement rules

As of June 22, USDA has confirmed 15 detections — 14 in Texas, 1 in New Mexico — the first U.S. cattle infestations since the parasite was eradicated in 1966. States beyond the outbreak zone are adding import restrictions and inspection protocols, so expect friction on interstate cattle movement, plus new wound-inspection and treatment costs if it keeps marching north.

Ag-Tech · Environment

First Milk maps water risk by satellite across all 700 member farms

The UK co-op is overlaying satellite topography mapping (field slope, proximity to watercourses) with two years of nutrient-footprint data to pinpoint manure and runoff risk farm-by-farm — and reports that lower-footprint farms held their milk-solids output. It is a scalable template for getting ahead of nutrient/water rules before regulators or processors impose them on you.

Regulatory Enforcement

An Ontario dairy is fined $270K after an unattended manure pump killed a creek

Henro Dairy Farms was convicted under the Ontario Water Resources Act after a manure pump left running caused a fish kill in Horner Creek; the penalty carried a mandatory 25% victim surcharge on top. The whole event traces to one routine task left unsupervised — a blunt illustration of the six-figure exposure sitting inside everyday manure handling.

Exports · China

a2 Milk clears China's SAMR to move formula registrations to its own brand — and teases a A$300M dividend

China's market regulator approved shifting two China-label infant-formula registrations (acquired with the Pokeno plant) onto the a2 brand, clearing the last gate on that deal and short-circuiting a normally long, uncertain registration process. The board signaled a A$300M special dividend — a read on how firms now value confirmed access to China's formula channel, which underpins demand for whole- and skim-milk powder.

Markets · Supply

The U.S. milk tide is still rising — herd up 184,000 head year-over-year

USDA's May data put output up 2.3% to 20.6 billion pounds, with the herd at 9.665 million cows (+10,000 from April, +184,000 vs. 2025); spot block cheddar slipped below $1.40/lb for the first time since February. Expanding supply meeting soft domestic demand is the bearish backdrop pressuring Class III revenue — the one number to keep tethering your hedging and cash-flow assumptions to.
