Animal Health · Science

01Why H5N1 hides in the udder — and how that fooled early testing

A new University of Pittsburgh study in Science Advances shows H5N1 latches onto N-linked sialic-acid receptors that are densely expressed in mammary tissue but scarce in cattle airways — explaining why the virus concentrates in milk and why respiratory swabs missed it for weeks in 2024. The mechanism validates milk-based surveillance and offers a screen for spotting the next surprise mammalian host.

Policy · Your Money

02Three farmers sue USDA to rein in the dairy checkoff

Wisconsin producers, backed by the Wisconsin Institute for Law & Liberty, filed suit June 10 arguing the mandatory 15¢/cwt checkoff unconstitutionally compels them to bankroll the Innovation Center for U.S. Dairy's net-zero and ESG programs. A win could reshape how checkoff dollars — money every U.S. producer pays on every hundredweight — are allowed to be spent.

Wisconsin Institute for Law & Liberty · June 2026

Labor

03DHS opens the H-2A door to year-round dairy labor

A Department of Homeland Security policy clarification now says dairy "can involve H-2A-eligible temporary or seasonal labor" even where milking runs year-round — a long-sought change as immigration enforcement tightens the available workforce. The catch: a seasonal visa framework still fits awkwardly over a 365-day operation, so how much real relief it delivers is unproven.

Wisconsin Watch · June 22, 2026

Regulatory · Inputs

04Executive order speeds new pesticide approvals, funds precision ag

A June 25 White House order directs EPA to fast-track registration of newer active ingredients and alternatives to older chemistries, while increasing federal funding for regenerative and precision-agriculture programs. For dairies, the levers that matter are crop and forage input costs, herbicide availability, and the economics of precision feeding and manure technology.

Trade · Labeling

05Lactalis takes Nutri-Score to the EU's top court over dairy penalties

France's Conseil d'État has referred Lactalis's challenge of the 2025 Nutri-Score algorithm to the Court of Justice of the EU; the company argues the revised scoring unfairly downgrades cheese and miscategorizes some dairy as drinks. A CJEU ruling would set the ground rules for front-of-pack labeling of dairy across Europe — a market that shapes global product and export strategy.

FoodNavigator · June 25, 2026

Feed · Cost

06Penn State: grocery-store waste can replace 15% of the ration

In a 48-cow Holstein trial, recovered supermarket produce and bakery waste substituted for corn and molasses at up to 15% of dry-matter intake with no loss in milk yield or components — and improved fiber and fat digestibility. Where a reclaimed-food supply chain exists, it's a concrete feed-cost lever rather than a sustainability talking point.

DairyReporter · June 26, 2026

Land · Competitive

07Data centers are now bidding against dairies for land, water and power

Reporting from this year's dairy-conference circuit describes AI data-center developers pushing some Upper-Midwest farmland toward roughly $23,000/acre and competing for the same flat ground, transmission capacity and water that dairy expansion depends on — squeezing both growth and succession planning. The paradox: that same digital build-out underpins robotic milking and herd analytics.

Genetics · Components

08India's bet: engineer milk to 6.8% fat to lift farmgate pay

India's National Institute of Animal Nutrition and Physiology, partnering with the Karnataka Milk Federation, has launched year-long field trials using targeted nutrition to raise butterfat and solids-not-fat and tie producers to higher component-based payouts. Worth tracking as component pricing increasingly drives the U.S. milk check, too.
