01Labor · Policy
The H-2A door opens to dairy — but with new scrutiny attached

DHS and the Labor Department issued guidance on June 17 clarifying that dairies can use the H-2A visa program when they show a genuine temporary or seasonal need — for example, filing separate petitions of up to 10 months around distinct calving seasons. It's the first real H-2A pathway for an industry long locked out by its year-round labor profile, but USCIS is signaling tighter review of what counts as "temporary," so audit and denial risk rise alongside the opportunity.

02Processing · Northeast
DFA to idle its St. Albans, Vermont plant; ~80 jobs end Aug. 17

Dairy Farmers of America will halt day-to-day production at its St. Albans creamery — a facility that has packed product tied to names like Ben & Jerry's and Cabot — keeping only a small on-site team and retaining ownership of the building. It's another exit from New England's shrinking processing footprint that reshuffles where regional milk gets a home and underscores the margin squeeze on older fluid and specialty plants.

03Herd Health · Biosecurity
New World screwworm returns to U.S. cattle as the border stays shut

APHIS confirmed New World screwworm in Texas calves in early June (Zavala County) — the first U.S. cattle cases in decades — and has begun twice-weekly sterile-fly releases while the southern border remains closed to Mexican cattle. For dairies that means continued tightness in feeder and replacement supply, plus a live threat to watch in fresh cows and the navels of newborn calves.

04Markets · Powder
Powder cracks: nonfat dry milk slides to $1.67

CME Grade A nonfat dry milk fell to roughly $1.67/lb by June 16, down from about $2.15 two weeks earlier — a sharp mid-month break in the most export-sensitive product on the board. Soft powder pulls on Class IV and hints at cooling international demand just as U.S. milk output climbs, a flag worth watching for second-half mailbox prices.

05Markets · Stocks
Cold-storage builds seasonally but stays under last year

USDA's latest Cold Storage report read neutral: cheese inventories edged up about 0.6% on the month to 1.413 billion pounds yet sit roughly 1.5% below 2024, while butter built to 361.6 million pounds but remains about 4.8% under a year ago. Tight year-over-year stocks keep a floor under cheese and butter even as production grows — a quiet support for Class III and IV.

06Context · The Big Picture
The milk check is now a beef check

A new industry analysis argues beef-calf revenue has climbed from roughly 5% to 20–25% of dairy income — to the point that "no beef equals no margin" — while GLP-1-driven protein demand and China's shifting diet reshape product mix. A useful read on how culling discipline, beef-on-dairy breeding, and component focus, not just the milk price, now drive the bottom line.
